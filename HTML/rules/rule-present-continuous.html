Present Continuous Tense

Used for actions happening now, temporary situations, and future arrangements

Formation

Type Structure Examples
Affirmative Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing I am working, She is working, They are working
Negative Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing I'm not working, She isn't working, They aren't working
Questions Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing? Are you working? Is she working? Are they working?

Spelling Rules for -ING Form

Rule Base Form -ING Form
Most verbs: add -ing work, play, read working, playing, reading
Verbs ending in -e: remove -e and add -ing write, take, make writing, taking, making
Verbs ending in -ie: change to -ying lie, die, tie lying, dying, tying
One-syllable verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant: double final consonant run, sit, stop running, sitting, stopping
Verbs ending in -w, -x, -y: just add -ing show, fix, play showing, fixing, playing

Usage

When to Use Examples Time Expressions
Actions happening now I'm reading a book right now. She's cooking dinner at the moment. now, right now, at the moment, currently
Temporary situations He's staying with friends this week. They're working on a special project. this week, these days, for now, temporarily
Changing situations Your English is improving. The weather is getting warmer. -
Future arrangements I'm meeting friends tonight. We're flying to Paris next week. tonight, tomorrow, next week, on Friday
Annoying habits (with always) He's always interrupting people. She's constantly losing her keys. always, constantly, forever

Stative Verbs (Not Usually Used in Continuous)

Category Verbs Examples
Thoughts & opinions know, believe, understand, think, agree I know the answer. (NOT I'm knowing)
Feelings & emotions like, love, hate, prefer, want She loves chocolate. (NOT She's loving)
Senses & perception see, hear, smell, taste, feel This tastes delicious. (NOT This is tasting)
Possession have, own, possess, belong I have a car. (NOT I'm having)
Other states be, seem, cost, need, mean He seems tired. (NOT He's seeming)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't use Present Continuous with stative verbs: I'm knowing → I know
  • Don't forget the -ing ending: I am work now → I am working now
  • Use correct form of 'be': He are working → He is working
  • Don't confuse with Present Simple for facts: Water is boiling at 100°C → Water boils at 100°C
