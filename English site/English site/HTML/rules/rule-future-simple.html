Future Simple Tense

Used for predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises, and future facts

Formation

Type Structure Examples
Affirmative Subject + will + base verb I will work, She will play, They will see
Negative Subject + will not (won't) + base verb I won't work, She won't play, They won't see
Questions Will + subject + base verb? Will you work? Will she play? Will they see?
Short Answers Yes/No + subject + will/won't Yes, I will. No, she won't.

Usage

When to Use Examples Time Expressions
Predictions It will rain tomorrow. She will pass the exam. tomorrow, next week, in the future, soon
Spontaneous decisions I'll have the steak. I'll help you with that. – (decisions made at the moment)
Promises I will always love you. We will finish the project on time. –
Offers and requests I'll carry your bags. Will you help me? –
Future facts The sun will rise at 6:23 AM. The concert will start in 10 minutes. –
Threats and warnings If you do that again, I'll tell the teacher. –

Alternative Future Forms

Form Usage Examples
Be going to Plans, intentions, and predictions based on evidence I'm going to visit my parents. Look at those clouds - it's going to rain.
Present Continuous Fixed arrangements and definite plans I'm meeting friends tonight. We're flying to Paris next week.
Present Simple Timetables and schedules The train leaves at 5 PM. The store opens at 9 AM.

Future Time Clauses

Structure Rule Examples
When/Before/After/As soon as + Present Simple Use present tense in time clauses I will call you when I arrive. She'll text you as soon as she finishes.
If + Present Simple, will + base verb First conditional for real future possibilities If it rains, we will cancel the picnic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't use 'will' in time clauses: When I will arrive → When I arrive
  • Use 'be going to' for plans, not 'will': I will study medicine → I'm going to study medicine (if it's a plan)
  • Don't forget the base form after 'will': I will to go → I will go
  • Use Present Simple for schedules: The train will leave at 5 PM → The train leaves at 5 PM
  • Don't use 'will' for arranged events: I will meet my boss at 3 PM → I'm meeting my boss at 3 PM
