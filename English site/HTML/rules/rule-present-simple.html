Present Simple Tense

Used for habits, general truths, repeated actions, and fixed arrangements

Formation

Type Structure Examples
Affirmative Subject + base verb (+ s/es for he, she, it) I work, She works, They play
Negative Subject + do/does not + base verb I don't work, She doesn't work
Questions Do/Does + subject + base verb? Do you work? Does she work?

Spelling Rules for Third Person Singular

Rule Base Form Third Person
Most verbs: add -s work, play, run works, plays, runs
Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -z: add -es watch, wash, pass, fix, buzz watches, washes, passes, fixes, buzzes
Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -es study, try, carry studies, tries, carries
Verbs ending in vowel + y: add -s play, stay, enjoy plays, stays, enjoys
Special cases: have, go, do have, go, do has, goes, does

Usage

When to Use Examples
Habits and routines I drink coffee every morning. She goes to the gym three times a week.
General truths and facts The sun rises in the east. Water boils at 100°C.
Permanent situations He works in a bank. They live in London.
Fixed arrangements and schedules The train leaves at 5 PM. The store opens at 9 AM.
With stative verbs I like chocolate. She knows the answer.

Time Expressions

  • Always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
  • Every day/week/month/year
  • On Mondays, at weekends
  • In the morning/afternoon/evening
  • Once a week, twice a month

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't forget the -s in third person singular: She work → She works
  • Don't use Present Simple for actions happening now: I eat lunch now → I'm eating lunch now
  • Use "do/does" for questions and negatives: She not work → She doesn't work

Practice Examples

Sentence Explanation
I usually get up at 7 AM. Habit/routine

He doesn't speak French. Negative form
